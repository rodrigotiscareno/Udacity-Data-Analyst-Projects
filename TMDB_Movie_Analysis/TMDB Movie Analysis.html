Introduction ¶

Online blogs, a friend's reccommendation, box office sales' figures... the list goes on and on. There are numerous ways to determine if a film was successful. What if there was a common pattern among films; patterns that could help in determining its success before its release date? In this data exploration, different movie characteristics will be analyzed to find patterns and spot correlations pertaining to production success.

The dataset that will be covered in this data exploration is the TMDB Movie Dataset from Kaggle, an open-source database.

Focus¶

The focus of the data analysis is centered around finding correlations between movie properties and their release success. Our basis of success will be metrics centred around the movie revenue, profit, and audience rating. These success metrics will act as the dependent variables in the data analysis. The indepdendent variables will consist of metrics such as movie genre, runtime, initial budget, director and crew involved included in the dataset.

The questions to be explored in this data analysis are:

  • What kind of movie properties, cast members, and production companies have resulted in higher profits?

Answering this question is essential to answering the main concept of this data analysis which is to examine the main independent variables and their impact on the dependent variable: profit.

  • Which genres require a higher budget and which ones have a higher runtime? What is the correlation between a movie's runtime and their budget?

Taking a closer dive into what which genres require the most allocation of budget. When it is understood which budgets drive the highest profit margins, understanding which genres require the highest budget indicates whether the genre is costly but also generating a lot of revenue. In addition, analyzing the genre's runtime can indicate whether runtime, which has a positive correlation to profit, can also be influenced with the genres.

  • What are the trends in movie productions over the past 10 years in terms of budget, profit, and fan ratings?

Understanding the trends in profit and initial budget can indicate whether technological advances has aided the profitability of movies. Has technology and movie advancements played a role in how profitable modern movies are? Do modern movies require more budget than historical films?

  • How does the release month relate to a movie's success?

Release month also acts as an independent variable to a movie's success. Deciphering whether each month has an impact on the average profitability of a film can help in determining if release month is a valid independent variable that has an effect in movie profit.

  • What are the top 10 most profitable movies?

The Top 10 most profitable movies provide little to no value to the data analysis but was of one's own curiosity.

Describing Finalized Fields¶

popularity: Numeric rating based on user interactivity with movie on TMDB website and platforms.
original_title: Title of the movie.
cast: Actors involved in the movie.
director: Director of the movie.
runtime: Total duration of the movie (minutes).
genres: The multiple genres of the movie.
production_companies: The production companies involved in the making of the movie.
vote_count: Number of votes cast for the movie rating.
vote_average: Average movie rating cast.
budget_adj: Total value invested into the production of the movie adjusted for inflation (2010).
revenue_adj: Total revenue acquired from the movie adjusted for inflation (2010).
profit: Total movie profit adjusted for inflation (2010).

Addressing Multiple Values for a Single Entry¶

The cast, genres, and production companies all have "|"-seperated values indicating a list of different values. We can correct this formatting using the get_dummies() function which allows for the values to be seperated into binary columns for each row. After attempting this direction, there were difficulties in converting the data into graphable categories. The one hot encoding is more suitable for machine learning applications. The explode() method seems like the right direction to take, which splits each created list item for each row into multiple values. The genre_df will be used to compare quantitative values to the categorical data of genres.

In [84]:
def seperateListings(originalDF, column_name):
    new_df = df.copy()
    new_df = new_df.dropna()
    new_df[column_name] = new_df[column_name].apply(lambda x : x.split("|"))
    new_df = new_df.explode(column_name)
    return new_df

def century_analysis(df, target_column):
    new_df = df.resample("10Y", on='release_date')[[target_column, 'release_date']].mean()
    new_df['year'] = new_df.index.year
    new_df.set_index('year', inplace=True)
    return new_df

Conclusion ¶

What kind of movie properties, cast members, and production companies have resulted in higher profits?¶

As observed from the graphs, the most profitable movies had a higher initial budget and a slightly a higher runtime. The top actors who have amassed the highest profits for the productions they were apart of were Harrison Ford, Tom Cruise, and Tom Hanks. Moreover, the companies with the highest average profits were Werner Bros, Universal Pictures, and Paramount Pictures. The categories with the highest average profit were adventure, fantasy, and family - two of which also require the highest average budget to produce.

Which genres require a higher budget and which ones have a higher runtime? What is the correlation between a movie's runtime and their budget?¶

The genres which had the highest average budget to produce were adventure, fantasy, and action. The genres with the highest average runtime were history, war, and western. There was a positive correlation between runtime and budget allocated to a production.

What are the trends in movie productions over the past 10 years in terms of budget, profit, and fan ratings?¶

Generally, the era between 1970-1990 produced the most profitable movies, with the 1980's being the highest. Since that era, the average profit acquired from movie's has declined. From the 1960's the initial budget allocation of movies has increased until 2000, where it experienced its peak and declined. The 2000-2020 era remains the highest era of movie budget allocation. Movie fan ratings over time have remained at a steady average rating of approximately 6.

How does the release month relate to a movie's success?¶

Movies released in June featured the highest average profit for movies with January being the least average profitable month. No seasonal or cyclical patterns were observed nor where there any correlation of release month with fan ratings.
